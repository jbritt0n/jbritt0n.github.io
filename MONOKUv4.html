🌸 What is Monoku?

Monoku is a poetic language for generative art. It lives inside a single HTML page and turns haiku into canvas strokes, weather into turbulence, and the length of a word into the weight of a mark.

Born from sumi‑e ink painting, haiku, and the generative art of Mark Webster, Monoku treats the online canvas as your private paper. Every line you write is a gesture; every verb, a loaded brush; every modifier, a breath of wind or a shift of light.

Philosophy: The canvas listens. You don't command; you whisper. A poem in Monoku has three breath‑phases: atmosphere (setting the paper, mood, season), gesture (the actual drawing verbs), and coda (a final veil, grain, or varnish).

Press ⌘↵ to render. Click ◎ to animate. Click ≈ to let the poem breathe line by line.

🎨 Glossary of Verbs

Over 80 painting verbs are available. Here are the families:

  • Canvas: breathe, prime, silence, fade
  • Wet media: wash, glaze, bleed, dew, rain, haze, veil, sfumato, chiaroscuro, blush, drip
  • Dry media: stipple, scumble, drybrush, etch, scratch, rake
  • Oil & impasto: impasto, scrape, smudge, alla prima
  • Organic marks: bloom, scatter, petal, branch, dapple, spiral, constellation, ripple, smoke, nebula, web, glyph
  • Transformative: burn, dissolve, erode, tarnish, tint, shade, underpaint, varnish, patina, foxing, burnish, crumble, crack, flick, pulse
  • Structure: echo, refrain, pause, linger, hurry, global, remember

Full list in the Verbs tab of the reference panel.

🎨 Glossary of Modifiers

Modifiers follow the colour and number in a command. Examples:

  • Opacity: soft, faint, dim, bold, hard, deep, bright, dark, light, pale
  • Distribution: gathered, clustered, scattered
  • Pace: slowly, patiently, gently, quickly, rapidly, frantic
  • Character: hushed, trembling, decisive, hesitant, firm, loose, brittle, fluid, aged, toothed, saturated, mottled, ghosted, weathered, viscous, lean
  • Ink load: loaded, spent, soaked, parched

📜 Syntax Rules

A Monoku line is a breath. It is read from top to bottom.

Basic Line Structure

verb colour [count] [modifiers…]

Example: bloom rose gathered soft

Placement

Use a comma to set where the command happens:

at the heart, bloom gold

Common placements: the heart, the horizon, the upper edge, in the light, a quiet corner, across the canvas.

Multiple gestures

Use — (em‑dash) to put several commands on one line:

bloom rose gathered — stroke gold soft

Loop and Condition

for 7 times, scatter leaves

if the mood is melancholy, veil violet

when the wind rises, drift ash

Variables

let sky be cerulean

set depth to 5

Then use them: for depth times, bloom sky

Gesture macros

define sunrise as:
  wash dawn
  bloom gold gathered
end

invoke sunrise

Global commands

global slowly (applies to all following lines)

the air is humid, the room is cold, the wind is rising, season winter, the feeling is wonder

Echo & Refrain

echo 3 bloom ivory

Poetic colour mixing

mix cerulean with snow

a ghost of rose

a memory of gold

a whisper of violet

a kiss of crimson on gold

Comments

Lines starting with — are titles.

Inline comments use ; – everything after is ignored.

Blank lines are breaths.

✨ Tips for New Users

  • Start small. One verb, one colour. Render. Then add another.
  • Use the chips. Click any verb, colour, or modifier in the reference panel to insert it into your scroll.
  • Experiment with paper. Try the paper is rice or the paper is aged to change how marks feel.
  • Let the mood help. the feeling is serenity gently shifts all colours toward calm tones.
  • Activate ≈ breath mode to watch your poem being painted line by line, as if recited.
  • Use echo liberally. echo 5 scatter gold creates beautiful, organic repetition.
  • Define your own gestures. If a certain combination of verbs feels right, wrap it in a define block and reuse it.
  • Haiku mode activates automatically when your poem has three active lines that roughly follow 5‑7‑5 syllables. The first line draws softly, the second boldly, the third fades.
  • If the canvas stumbles, the error message will appear as a soft studio critique, not a scary red box.
  • Save your work. Click save png to keep the moment.
